Tom Zé Tribute

The Last Tropicalian

Tom Zé, singer and composer. Always emblematic and with so much to say.

Here's a time line of Dr. Tom Zé's life:

  • 1936 - Born in Irará, Bahia - Brazil
  • 50's -
  • Earl 60's - Start to study music in Bahia Music University, where later years later he would become a harmony professor.
  • 1968 - Earn the fourth Brazilian Popular Music Festival with the song "São Paulo, meu amor". That was a turning point of his career. Also in this year, released his first album Tom Zé - Grande Liquidação.
  • 1970 - Release of his second album "Tom Zé", one of the most acclaimed of his career.
  • 1973 - Release of his third album "Todos os olhos". Probably the most controversial in his career, as it was censored by the totalitary brazilian's government because of the front cover.
  • 1976 - > Release of his fourth album "Ensinando o Samba". Here, the most clear signal that he was taking the experimental music direction, which would be his career choice from this time on.
  • Latest 80's - David Byrne (Talking Heads) in a trip to Brazil, discovers Tom Zé accidentally and get very impressed. This acossiation was a re-emergy for Tom Ze's career. He released a compilation and two albums, all of which received positive reviews from critics in the United States.
